Reliability and reproducilbility are very important for connectomics research, particularly if we are going to translate scientific findings into useful clinical biomarkers and treatment targets. Although a variety of studies have investigated these aspects of Connectomes analyses from different perspectives, there is no consensus on the current state of reproducibility for connectomics research or what needs to be done to improve it.

Brainhack Vienna, will be held just prior to this years’ Conference on Resting State Brain Connectivity and will provide a unique venue for investigating this important topic. The three-day event will occur September 18 – 20 at the ImpactHUB co-working space in Vienna and will feature open discussions and collaborative sessions related to assessing and improving the reproducibility of functional and diffusion MRI based Connectomes analyses.

Full-length editorial and review papers developed from the discussions that occur at the event will be invited for publication in the Brainhack Series of the Gigascience Journal. Project reports describing tools and analyses will be invited for publication in the 2016 Brainhack Proceedings.

Registration covers lunch and refreshments for each day and attendance at a reception on Monday night.

Fifth Biennial Conference on Resting State and Brain Connectivity



Brainhack Vienna will preceed the the Fifth Biennial Conference on Resting State and Brain Connectivity, which will take place in Vienna (Austria) on September 21 to 23, 2016.

The organizing committee brings together key researchers working on technical advances and methodological issues in imaging the brain’s functional connectome. In addition to the major topics of earlier years, the Fifth Biennial Conference on Resting State / Brain Connectivity will include sessions on multi-modal imaging approaches to measure brain structure as well as brain function and include human imaging methods and animal models. Approximately one third of the conference will be dedicated to applications of brain connectivity methods and models in neurological and psychiatric disease.

The venue of the meeting will be the University of Vienna. With a long-standing tradition of over 650 years, the University of Vienna is one of the oldest universities in Europe. It is located along the Wiener Ringstrasse, which surrounds the historical city center. The social event will take place at the Orangerie of Schönbrunn Palace.

The conference is preceded by an educational workshop on 19-20 September providing the background to state-of-the-art resting state and brain connectivity research. For the third time we now offer this intensive pre-conference workshop for those aiming to maximize their individual outcome for the main conference.

For more information go to www.restingstate.com.
